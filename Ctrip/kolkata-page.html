Welcome to Kolkata

Kolkata, the cultural capital of India, where history meets modernity in a vibrant tapestry of art, cuisine, and heritage.

Top 10 Tourist Attractions in Kolkata

Victoria Memorial

A symbol of Kolkata's colonial heritage, the Victoria Memorial is a magnificent marble building dedicated to Queen Victoria. It houses a museum with a vast collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts from the British era.

Howrah Bridge

An iconic symbol of Kolkata, the Howrah Bridge is one of the busiest cantilever bridges in the world. It spans the Hooghly River and is a bustling thoroughfare for vehicles, pedestrians, and street vendors.

Dakshineswar Kali Temple

Located on the eastern bank of the Hooghly River, the Dakshineswar Kali Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Kali. It was built in the 19th century and is known for its intricate architecture and spiritual significance.

Belur Math

The headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission, Belur Math is a serene and spiritual complex. It combines elements of Hindu, Islamic, and Christian architecture and promotes the teachings of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahansa.

Indian Museum

Established in 1814, the Indian Museum is one of the oldest and largest museums in India. It houses a diverse collection of artifacts, including ancient sculptures, fossils, paintings, and cultural exhibits.

Amrapali Museum

Amrapali Museum is one of the popular museums of Jaipur known for showcasing different styles of Indian Jewellery art. It houses a wide collection of Tribal jewellery along with Mughal style, Rajput style and temple jewellery.

Ram Niwas Garden

Ram Niwas Garden is a royal garden built in 1868 by Maharaja Sawai Ram Singh. A historical landmark, the Albert Hall Museum is located here. Explore the lush green landscaped gardens and or visit the theatre, the Bird Park, the Zoo or the Art Gallery. There are also several cafes located nearby.

Swaminarayan Temple

Dedicated to the Hindu God, Narayan Akshardham Temple is known for its beautiful architecture, magnificent idols, sculptures, and carvings. The lush green surroundings add to its beauty and serenity.

Jaigarh Fort

Jaigarh Fort is a grand structure perched on the top of the 'Cheel ka teela' hills designed by a talented architect called Vidhyadhar. The fort reflects the city's prosperous past and is named after the ruler who got it built, Sawai Jai Singh II. It is connected to Amer Fort through subterranean passages. The fort currently houses the world's largest cannon on wheels - 'Jaivana' and offers a magnificent view of Jaipur city.

Jal Mahal

Situated amidst Man Sagar Lake, Jal Mahal is a stunning water palace known for its picturesque setting. While the palace itself is not open to the public, visitors can enjoy the view from the banks of the lake.
